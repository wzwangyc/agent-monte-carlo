\noindent
\textbf{Why This Paper Matters:}

\vspace{0.2cm}

\noindent
Financial markets exhibit striking empirical regularities---fat tails, volatility clustering, and endogenous crashes---that traditional Monte Carlo models fail to reproduce. This paper establishes Agent Monte Carlo, a simulation framework that combines the computational efficiency of Monte Carlo methods with the behavioral richness of agent-based modeling.

\vspace{0.3cm}

\noindent
\textbf{Key Contributions:}

\vspace{0.2cm}

\noindent
\textit{Theoretical:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item First proof of equilibrium existence in financial ABM with EWA learning
    \item Complete comparative statics framework with explicit formulas
    \item Convergence rate analysis with phase diagram
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.2cm}

\noindent
\textit{Empirical:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Calibration to 44 years of S\&P 500 data with bootstrap standard errors
    \item Reproduction of 5+ stylized facts with $<10\%$ error
    \item International validation across 5 markets (US, UK, Japan, Germany, China)
    \item Model comparison showing superiority over GARCH and Stochastic Volatility
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.2cm}

\noindent
\textit{Policy:}
\begin{itemize}

    \item Identification of optimal policy combination (leverage caps 5--10x)
    \item Robustness to welfare weight assumptions
    \item Implementation roadmap for regulators
\end{itemize}
